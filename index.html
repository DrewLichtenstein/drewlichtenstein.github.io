Drew Lichtenstein's Portfolio

I am a professional project manager and instructional designer, having worked with nearly a dozen Harvard faculty to bring their online courses to over a million learners. Additionally, I have extensive knowledge of front-end web design, including HTML, CSS, and JavaScript, as well as having worked closely with graphic designers and video editors to help visualize and bring to life academic content.

Justice

I lead the redesign for world renowned philosopher Michael Sandel's Justice, a course that has enrolled hundreds of thousands of learners on edX. Under my stewardship, the course was updated with interactive tools, animated moral dilemmas, new assessments, and translations in five different languages.

View the course live on edX.org

I oversaw the production of nearly 20 animated moral dilemmas, the first new material added to the course since Professor Sandel originally filmed the classroom lectures.

The moral dilemmas are paired with a provocative poll questions which students answer, give a reason for their answer, see a selection of their fellow students' reasons, and then finally choose which reason they think is best -- a seamless way to include interaction in a large, a synchronous course.

PredictionX

I am the project lead for Professor Alyssa Goodman's PredictionX, a project that looks at human attempts to divine the future from ancient religions to modern computer simulations. To date, we have collaborated with nearly 30 experts from Harvard and beyond to create three edX modules, an open website, and an on-campus courae at Harvard.

View content on predictionx.org or on one of our three current edX modules (with more coming soon): Diviner's Guide, John Snow and the Cholera Outbreak of 1854, and Lost Without Longitude.

I am the lead producer for the project's videos, including this exceptional video on John Snow and the cholera outbreak of 1854, which combines on-location footage and in-house graphics to clearly and engagingly tell a story of a scientific breakthrough. Additionally, the same content is presented in the edX platform with a video annotaitnon tool, which allows for additional depth and context for the learner.

The PredictionX series of modules on edX has had particular need for customization in the edX platform, including making custom image sliders, offsetting text, and other tricks for adding functionality to the platform. To that end, I have implemented and adapted in-house, open source CSS and JavaScript for extending edX's functionality.

CitiesX

I was the lead producer for one of HarvardX's largest projects yet -- a course adapation of Professor Edward Glaeser's best selling book about cities, which includes over 160 videos, including location shoots in Brazil and England. I worked with a fellow HarvardX project to lead to oversee the pre-production, filming logistics, post-production editing, and implementation of the course on edX -- we completed this massive project under time and videos at an exceptionally high quality for the number we needed to produce.

Sign up for the course on edX or check out all the videos on the course's YouTube channel.

High quality videos include a mix of location and studio shoots; I worked with Professor Glaeser on scripting and then personally did image researching and paper edits for the majority of videos.

Despite being video heavy, we made sure that the course was still a relevant learning experience for students and not just a series of videos. To that end, I oversaw the writing of pedagogically-sound multiple choice questions (no option too obvious or difficult)-- one for every video.

Other Projects

I have worked on several other projects at Harvard, ranging from internal-facing courses to academic research projects.

I work on a team that produces Harvard Law School's Zero-L, a prematriculation course for incoming first year Harvard Law School students. The course was very positively received in its pilot year and we added new modules for the 2019 re-launch.

With my colleagues, I was awarded a $15,000 HILT Spark Grant in which we studied faculty's experiences teaching online; our results show that faculty who teach online find that it makes them feel like a better teacher in general, both for the online audience and their on-campus students.

Beyond my work at HarvardX, I am a passionate educator -- I have Master's in International Education Policy from the Harvard Graduate School of Education and I spent two years in Shanghai, China teaching Advance Placement history and literature courses to students who were studying to come to the United States for university (and where I also learned to speak and write Chinese at a fairly high level).
